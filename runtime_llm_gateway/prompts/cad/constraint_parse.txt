Parse the user's design request into structured system constraints.

Extract:
1. systems: which engineering systems are involved
   - "mechanical": housing, modules, moving parts, joints
   - "electrical": wiring, connectors, PCB, sensors, power
   - "plumbing": drainage, water supply, sealing, fluid paths
   - "structure": load-bearing, wall thickness, reinforcement
   - "exterior": form factor, surface finish, aesthetics
2. constraints: list of specific constraints with severity
   - constraint_type: what kind (dimension, clearance, material, interface, environmental)
   - severity: "critical" (must satisfy), "high" (strong preference), "normal" (nice to have), "low" (optional)
   - details: specific values, ranges, or conditions (REQUIRED when severity is "critical" or "high")
3. priorities: ordered list of what matters most
   - e.g., ["waterproof_integrity", "compact_size", "easy_maintenance"]

Rules:
- "심플" = prioritize simplicity, fewer modules, clean exterior
- "배수 고려" = add plumbing system with drainage constraints
- "전기선도 같이" = add electrical system
- If user mentions "방수" → critical constraint with IP rating
- Structural constraints are always at least "normal" severity
- Manufacturing method affects ALL system constraints (injection molding vs 3D print)
